RESEARCH

Research topic

Cultural, Social, and Personality Psychology?

Korea University's Cultural, Social, and Personality Psychology is the only university in Korea to study cultural psychology, social and personality psychology within one major, providing an opportunity to combine the contents with each other. We study social psychology that deals with the incidents that overwhelm individuals and personality psychology that deals with the power of individuals going through situations in a cultural context. It deals with a wide range of topics, from the unique psychology of Koreans and the psychology of the East and West to the comparative cultural psychology, social cognition and emotion, altruism and happiness, self and identity, etc. Studies on the following major fields are currently being conducted.

    Sun W. Park Associate Professor

    What is the power that drives an individual's life? Someone wants to find the answer to why they live a happy and healthy life with their full potential, others live a life that doesn't improve no matter how hard they try, and someone else lives without even striving for a better life. The answer to this question can be presented from many angles, but we are working on a number of topics related to identifying those perspective at their core.

    Self, identity, narrative identity, materialism, growth motivation

    Eun Soo Choi Assistant Professor

    Emotion, cognition. As psychological processes are formed in a cultural context, cultural and psychological processes are complex and closely related. Through a cross-cultural approach, we study how cultural self, norms, and beliefs relate to individual emotional/psychological processes. In particular, we are interested in psychological well-being, happiness, and meaning as a measure of an individual's adaptive functioning and living in a culture. In the future, we plan to study with particular attention to the psychological/physical health that appears in the process of interacting with the Korean cultural context of various groups and individuals (immigrants, multiculturalism, minorities) in Korean society.

    Culture, emotion, well-being

    Tae Kyun Heo PhD

    In social psychology, social cognition is the main research field, and studies on social judgment, misunderstanding, and post-family accidents were mainly conducted. Recently, based on the psychological characteristics of Koreans, various social psychological studies have been conducted not only on the differences in the thinking process according to cultures but also on the judicial judgment process and leisure psychology to find out the psychological meaning of leisure.

    Societal Psychology (social cognition, culture), Social judgment and misconception, Specific Korean cultural thinking characteristics, Judicial judgment
