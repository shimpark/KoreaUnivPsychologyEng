RESEARCH

Research topic

Behavioral Cognitive Neuroscience?

The Department of Behavioral Cognitive Neuroscience at Korea University encompasses biopsychology and cognitive psychology, and is the only domestic psychology field with specialized expertise in brain science research. Comprehensive research topics on cognitive processes ranging from perception, attention, memory, emotions, language, and decision-making, adopting various neuroscientific approaches from electrophysiology methods for animals to brain imaging methods for humans. There are currently six professors, and research is being conducted on the following major fields.

  • more

    Chae Yeon Kim PhD

    How will the various information entered through sensory organs such as eyes, nose, mouth, ears and skin be experienced by us? And what is the neural basis that makes the experience possible? Focusing on the visual perception experience and its neural mechanisms, we study the interactions with other senses. From basic information such as points, lines, colors, and tones to information containing social meanings such as faces, gestures, and voices, and further to complex artistic information such as art and music, perceptual processing of a wide range of stimuli. Through this, we are researching industrial and social applications, such as design that considers human perception experiences.

    Perception, Cognitive Neuroscience, Multisensory Interaction, Visual Awareness, Neuroaesthetics
  • more

    Hak Jin Kim PhD

    Can we define human emotions biologically? We aim to understand how various emotions arising from relationships with others, such as a need for recognition, self-esteem, empathy, morality, group conflict, and altruism, are linked to decision-making, mainly by using functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) and computational modeling techniques.

    Social neuroscience, Decision neuroscience, Neuroeconomics, Self-esteem, Empathy, Morality, Altruism
  • more

    Ki Jun Nam PhD

    Among cognitive psychology, the field of language psychology is not yet equipped with an academic foundation in Korea, so we are doing extensive research on Korean language processing to lay the foundation for it. Research on artificial intelligence is also underway, and projects related to emotional engineering (emotion measurement and standardization technology for content functionality evaluation) have been conducted. Recently, research on the improvement of brain function and the direction of socialization by combining it with IT are being conducted.

    Psycholinguistics, Human Learning and Memory, Information & Communications Technology for Language, Mind, and Cognition
  • more

    Yang Seok Cho PhD

    In order to reveal the characteristics of cognitive mechanisms related to cognitive control and attention, we analyze behavioral characteristics and brain responses using experimental methods in humans. In other words, we examine the characteristics of cognitive control, the role of attention, and variables that influence the attention process, and how cognitive processing changes adaptively according to the task environment or context. In response selection, another major research area, we try to find out how the response selection appears differently depending on the task goal and the type of stimulus among alternative responses to stimuli, and what cognitive mechanisms may be underlying it. In addition, the focus is on human factors that facilitate the convenience of people who use multiple products based on how people perceive and respond to the stimuli.

    Attention, Cognitive Control, Response Selection, User Experience, Emotion
  • more

    Yong Sang Cho PhD

    In the field of biopsychology or behavioral neuroscience, we study the functional connections and operating principles between specific brain regions and behaviors in mice. In the current laboratory, animal experiments are conducted on the subject of 'The effect of the dopamine system in the midbrain on the elimination of fear memory,’ 'Elimination of addictive behavior using the neural circuit of pain,' and 'The effect of the lateral rein nucleus on the quality and quantity of sleep.' The main research methods are to observe the behavior of animals after drug administration, genetic manipulation, photo/chemical activation manipulation, or conduct brain-behavioral research using techniques to record neuronal activity in living animals.

    Acquisition and extinction of fear memory, addictive behavior, neural circuitry of sleep
  • more

    Jun Shik Choi PhD

    We are studying the cognitive and emotional mechanisms of animals by developing animal models using robots. The main research includes research on compensation calculation and value-mapping in conflict situations using intra-brain drug injection and recording of neuronal activity. In addition, studies that mathematically explain and predict the learning and defense behaviors of animals, studies on defense behaviors according to gender and hormonal cycles through a self-developed fear learning model, and studies on the effects of trauma experiences on fear stimulus processing are in progress.

    Learning and Memory, Emotion Neuroscience, Robot Psychology
